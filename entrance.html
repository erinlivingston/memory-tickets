Enter Select Ticket About Microarchive Color

Tickets are scraps of our movement & time, and our transactions & consumption.

Once a material piece of travel memory, now, part of digital fabrics that form global infrastructures & interfaces.

This project plays with digital ticketing from commuter rail and explores a collection of ticket screenshots as mnemonic objects. Enter the train to subvert color and time, and to consider an embodied digital travel memory.

Trains have a form of time all their own.

In physics, trains are a crucial example of Relative Time, a part of Einstein's theory of relativity. It means, simply put, that time depends on our movement in & through space.

On the contemporary train, this relative time becomes a digital interface, used to transact, surveille, and erase riders to the rail's benefit. It extracts departure and destination, and categorizes our space and movement into tiers of profit.

During these slots, our to and from home, time in the middle is forgotten. Where do we go in our minds to escape these in-between moments? Why do we leave them behind?
