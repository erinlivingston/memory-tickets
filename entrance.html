Tickets are scraps and receipts of our movement & time, and our transactions & consumption. Tickets were once a material piece of travel memory. Now, they are part of digital fabrics that form global infrastructures & interfaces. This project plays with the digital fabric of a commuter rail, and explores its ticketing interface. Enter the train and consider embodied digital travel memory.

Trains have a form of time all their own. In physics, trains are a crucial example of Relative Time, a part of Einstein's theory of relativity. It means, simply put, that time depends on our movement in & through space.
